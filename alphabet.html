📚 About the Urdu Alphabet

Urdu uses a modified Perso-Arabic script with 38 letters. Key features:

  • ✅ Written from right to left ←
  • ✅ Letters change shape based on position
  • ✅ Most letters connect to the next
  • ✅ Vowels shown with diacritical marks

Learning Tip: Start with basic forms, then learn positional variants!

Letter Positions

🔄 Letters Change Shape!

Most Urdu letters have 4 forms depending on their position:

Example: بے (Be)

Position Form Name
Isolated ب Standing alone
Initial بـ Beginning of word
Medial ـبـ Middle of word
Final ـب End of word

Example Word: کتاب (kitaab - book) - ک (initial) - ت (medial) - ا (medial) - ب (final)

Similar Looking Letters

⚠️ Don’t Confuse These!

Pay attention to dots - they distinguish letters:

Letter Name Dots Sound
ب Be 1 below b
ت Te 2 above t
ث Se 3 above s/th
ن Noon 1 above n
ی Ye 2 below y

Practice Tip: Write these letters side by side to see differences!

Connecting Letters

🔗 Connection Rules

Connectors: Most letters connect to the next letter

Non-Connectors: These 6 letters don’t connect to the next: - ا (Alif) - د (Daal)
- ڈ (Ḍaal) - ذ (Zaal) - ر (Re) - ڑ (Ṛe) - ز (Ze) - ژ (Zhe) - و (Wao)

Example: - درد (dard - pain): د + ر + د (all separate) - باب (baab - chapter): ب + ا + ب (ا breaks connection)

Vowels & Diacritics

Short Vowels (حرکات)

Urdu uses marks above/below letters for short vowels:

Mark Name Sound Example
َ Zabar a (as in cat) کَ = ka
ِ Zer i (as in sit) کِ = ki
ُ Pesh u (as in put) کُ = ku
ْ Jazm No vowel کْ = k

Note: In everyday writing, these marks are often omitted. Learners should practice with them!
